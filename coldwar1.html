01 / BRIEFING

What was the Cold War?

It was not a war of tanks and trenches between the two biggest powers. It was a war of ideas, fear, and influence — fought through spies, speeches, space rockets, and smaller wars around the world.

Two Superpowers

After World War II, only the United States and the Soviet Union were strong enough to lead the world. A superpower (a very strong country) can shape other nations.

A War of Ideas

The USA believed in capitalism (free markets and private business). The USSR believed in communism (the government controls the economy). Each wanted the world to follow its ideology (system of ideas).

No Direct Fighting

They never fought each other directly. Both had nuclear weapons (bombs that could destroy cities). Instead, they fought proxy wars (wars through other countries) in Korea, Vietnam, and Afghanistan.

A Divided World

Europe split in two. Winston Churchill called this line the Iron Curtain. On the west side: democracy and free markets. On the east side: one-party states and state control.

02 / WORLDVIEWS

Two Visions of Freedom, Two Visions of Justice.

Each side believed it was fighting for a better world. Understanding both worldviews is the key to understanding the Cold War.

★ WESTERN BLOC · USA & ALLIES
Capitalism
LIBERAL DEMOCRACY · FREE MARKET
Economy: Private businesses compete. People own companies, land, and property.
Government: Many parties. Citizens vote in free elections to choose leaders.
The Individual: Individual freedom and rights come first. Free speech is protected.
Media: A free press can criticize the government without fear.
Religion: People may follow any religion, or none at all.
☭ EASTERN BLOC · USSR & ALLIES
Communism
ONE-PARTY STATE · PLANNED ECONOMY
Economy: The state owns factories, farms, and land. The government plans production.
Government: One party (the Communist Party) rules. No real opposition allowed.
The Individual: The group (the workers, the state) comes before the individual.
Media: The state controls newspapers, TV, and radio. Criticism is punished.
Religion: Religion is discouraged. The state promotes atheism.
